Leo Tolstoy - a mirror of the Russian revolution.

A group of secret admirers of the count.

  • Author: Leo Tolstoy
  • Publication Date: July 30, 1854

[Rymnik.] Rode to Rymnik on horseback. [[5]] The old man still doesn't bow to me. Both of these things irritate me. Behaved well with the encountered bashi-bazouks. Had a conversation with Kryzhanovsky. He, for reasons unknown to me, advises me to be attached to the Cossack battery; advice I won't follow. Argued bitterly in the evening with Frieda and Babarykin, scolded Sergeputovsky, and accomplished nothing. These are the three reproaches I have for myself for today. (3)

  • Author: Leo Tolstoy
  • Publication Date: July 29, 1854

My correction is going splendidly. I feel how my relationships become pleasant and easy with people of all kinds, ever since I decided to be modest and convinced myself that it is not necessary to always appear majestic and infallible. I am very cheerful. And may God grant, as it seems to me, that this joy comes from within myself; from the desire to be pleasant to everyone, from modesty, lack of offense, and attentiveness to flashes. Then I would always be cheerful and almost always happy. In the morning, I had decided to stay at home, work, but things didn't go well, and I couldn't resist going out for a walk in the evening. On the way back from dinner, Tishkevich and I stopped at the brothel and Kryzhanovsky caught us, which, I admit, was not quite pleasant to me. I reproach myself for being characterless, for not staying at home, and for the idleness of the whole day. That is the main point. (2)

  • Author: Leo Tolstoy
  • Publication Date: July 28, 1854

I am writing in the most pleasant and cheerful mood in which I spent the entire evening. In the morning, I read, ate pears, and instead of lunch, I had cheese. Despite the festivities of Stalypin and Sergeputovsky on the occasion of receiving awards, I did not envy them and had a cheerful day. In the evening, I took off my shoes and drank two glasses of champagne with Schwartz, Veilerovsky, and Gambich, then chatted with Shubin and Sasha Gorchakov. Excluding the indulgence, I am very pleased with my day. (1)
