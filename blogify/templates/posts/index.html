  • Author: Leo Tolstoy
  • Publication Date: July 30, 1854

[Rymnik.] Made a transition to Rymnik on horseback. [[5]] The old man still doesn't bow to me. Both of these things annoy me. Behaved well with encountered bashi-bazouks. Had a conversation with Kryzhanovsky. He, for some reason, advises me to be seconded to the Cossack battery; advice that I won't follow. Argued heatedly in the evening with Frieda and Babarykin, scolded Sergey Putovsky, and didn't accomplish anything. These are the three reproaches I have for myself for today. (3)

  • Author: Leo Tolstoy
  • Publication Date: July 29, 1854

My correction is going splendidly. I feel how my relationships become pleasant and easy with people of all kinds, since the time I decided to be modest and convinced myself that always appearing majestic and infallible is not necessary. I am very cheerful. And God grant, as it seems to me, that this cheerfulness comes from within me; from the desire to be pleasant to everyone, modesty, non-offensiveness, and attentiveness to flashes. Then I would always be cheerful and almost always happy. In the morning, I had decided to stay at home, study, but things didn't go well and I couldn't resist going out in the evening to wander. On the way back from dinner, Tishkevich and I stopped by the brothel and Kryzhanovsky caught us, which, I admit, was not entirely pleasant for me. I blame myself for being indecisive and not staying at home, and for idleness throughout the day. That's the main point. (2)

  • Author: Leo Tolstoy
  • Publication Date: July 28, 1854

I am writing in a very pleasant and cheerful state of mind, in which I spent the entire evening. In the morning, I read, had pears for lunch, and instead of a proper meal, I ate cheese. Despite the celebrations at Stalypin's and Sergey Putovsky's on the occasion of receiving awards, I didn't envy, but had a cheerful day. In the evening, I grabbed my slippers, had two glasses of champagne with Schwartz, Veilerovsky [?], and Hembich, then chatted with Shubin and Sasha Gorchakov. Apart from the festivities, I am very satisfied with my day. (1)
